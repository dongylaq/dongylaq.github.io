Hello! I am Yulong Dong, a PhD student at University of California, Berkeley. I got my BS in Chemical Physics with honor at University of Science and Technology of China (USTC) in 2018.

My undergraduate research was mainly about soft condensed matter and nonequilibrium dynamics. I did my research on active stochastic engine with Prof. Zhonghuai Hou at USTC. In the summer 2017, I conducted a summer research about nonequilibrium dynamics of protein bistability with Prof. Bin Zhang at MIT. In my first semester at Berkeley, I still worked on complex system and proposed an entropy-regularized stochastic control scheme to optimize control protocol for soft condensed matter. In March 2019, I started my research in quantum world! I am coadvised by Prof. Lin Lin in Math and Prof. Birgitta Whaley in Chemistry. I will develop numerical algorithms in quantum control and quantum information.

I am interested in computer science. Actually, I learned many computer science courses in my undergraduate university but I didn't finish this minor. I learned stochastic analysis in Prof. Shuguang Zhang's class which consolidated my understanding on probability theory and made me interested in stochastic control, therefore specifically, reinforcement learning.
