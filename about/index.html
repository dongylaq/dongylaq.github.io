About Me

Hello! I am Yulong Dong, a PhD student at University of California, Berkeley. I got my BS in Chemical Physics with honor at University of Science and Technology of China (USTC) in 2018.

My undergraduate research was mainly about soft condensed matter and nonequilibrium dynamics. I did my research on active stochastic engine with Prof. Zhonghuai Hou at USTC. In the summer 2017, I conducted a summer research about multiscale modeling of protein bistability with Prof. Bin Zhang at MIT. Before I got advisership from my current advisors at Berkeley, I still considered on complex systems and investigated stochastic control algorithms in soft condensed matter. In March 2019, I started my journey on quantum computation after discussion with Prof. Lin Lin in Berkeley Math. I am coadvised by Prof. Lin Lin in Math and Prof. Birgitta Whaley in Chemistry. My current research focuses on quantum signal processing, quantum linear systems problem and robust control in quantum theory.
